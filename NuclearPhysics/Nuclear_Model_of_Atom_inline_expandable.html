1. The Atom

What is an Atom?

Atoms are the building blocks of all matter. They consist of a small, dense, positively charged nucleus surrounded by negatively charged electrons in orbit.

(Please refer to the diagram of atomic structure in your notes!)

How big is an Atom?

Atoms are incredibly small, with a radius of only 1 x 10-10 m.

About 100 million atoms could fit side-by-side across your thumbnail!

How does the nucleus compare to the whole atom?

  • The nucleus contains almost all of the mass of the atom.
  • Its radius (~10-15 m) is over 10,000 times smaller than the atom's radius.

2. The Nucleus

Key Numbers

Proton Number (Z): The number of protons in an atom. Also called the Atomic Number.

Nucleon Number (A): The total number of protons and neutrons. Also called the Mass Number.

Neutrons = A - Z

Nuclide Notation

A Z
X

A = Nucleon no.

Z = Proton no.

X = Symbol

Worked Example: Analyzing an Atom

For the atom 235 92 U (Uranium-235), find the number of protons, neutrons, and electrons.

  1. Protons: The proton number (Z) is the bottom number. So, there are 92 protons.
  2. Electrons: In a neutral atom, the number of electrons equals the number of protons. So, there are 92 electrons.
  3. Neutrons: Use the formula Neutrons = A - Z.
    235 - 92 = 143 neutrons.

What are Isotopes?

Atoms of the same element with the:

  • Same number of protons (Z)
  • Different number of neutrons (A)

Worked Example: Isotopes of Chlorine

Chlorine exists as 35 17 Cl and 37 17 Cl . Show they are isotopes.

  • Both have 17 protons (so they are both Chlorine).
  • 35 Cl has 35 - 17 = 18 neutrons.
  • 37 Cl has 37 - 17 = 20 neutrons.
  • Since they have the same number of protons but different numbers of neutrons, they are isotopes.

4. Nuclear Fission & Fusion

Nuclear Fission

  • Definition: The splitting of a large, unstable nucleus into two smaller nuclei. This can be spontaneous or induced.
  • Process: A large nucleus (like Uranium) absorbs a neutron, becomes unstable, and splits.
  • Products: This releases two smaller nuclei, more neutrons, and a HUGE amount of energy.
  • Chain Reaction: The released neutrons can cause other nuclei to split.
    • Controlled reactions are used in nuclear power stations.
    • Uncontrolled reactions result in an atomic bomb.
    • (Refer to the chain reaction diagram in your notes!)

Nuclear Fusion

  • Definition: When two light nuclei join (fuse) together to form a heavier nucleus.
  • Energy Source: This is the process that powers stars, including our Sun!
  • Mass & Energy: The mass of the final nucleus is less than the mass of the original nuclei. The lost mass is converted into a VAST amount of energy.
  • Energy Scale: 1 kg of hydrogen fusing releases the same energy as burning about 10 million kg of coal.

Worked Example: Fission Equation

Calculate the number of neutrons (N) emitted in this fission reaction:

23592U + 10n ‚Üí 9637Rb + 13755Cs + N( 10n)

  1. Balance Nucleon (Mass) Numbers: The top numbers on both sides must be equal.
  2. Left side: 235 + 1 = 236
  3. Right side: 96 + 137 + (N x 1) = 233 + N
  4. Equate and Solve for N:
  5. 236 = 233 + N
  6. N = 236 - 233 = 3
  7. So, 3 neutrons are emitted.
