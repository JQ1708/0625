Exploring the properties and behaviors of matter at a particle level.

Properties of Solids

  • Definite shape and volume
  • Cannot flow
  • Not compressible
  • Particles held in a fixed lattice
  • Vibrate about fixed positions
  • Strong forces of attraction between particles

Properties of Liquids

  • Indefinite shape, definite volume
  • Can flow to take container's shape
  • Not compressible
  • Particles are closely packed but move randomly
  • Slide past each other
  • Weaker forces of attraction than solids

Properties of Gases

  • Indefinite shape and volume
  • Fills any container
  • Highly compressible
  • Particles are far apart and move randomly
  • Move rapidly and collide with each other and container walls
  • Negligible forces of attraction

Brownian Motion

This is the random movement of particles (e.g., smoke, pollen) suspended in a fluid (a liquid or a gas). It provides evidence for the Kinetic Particle Model.

It is caused by the much smaller, invisible particles of the fluid colliding with the larger, visible particle from all sides. These collisions are random and uneven, causing the larger particle to move erratically.

Internal Energy

The internal energy of a substance is the total energy of its molecules. It's the sum of two types of energy:

  • Kinetic Energy: Due to the motion of the particles. Related to temperature.
  • Potential Energy: Stored in the forces of attraction between particles. Related to the state and separation of particles.

Heating a substance increases its internal energy by increasing the kinetic and/or potential energy of its particles.

Gas Pressure & Temperature

For a gas at constant volume, increasing the temperature increases the pressure.

Explanation: Higher temperature gives the gas particles more kinetic energy. They move faster, leading to more frequent and more forceful collisions with the container walls, which increases the average pressure.
